The problem

You don't have a focus problem

Most blockers keep you focused on work. This one keeps you away from it.

You're not distracted. You're obsessed. You love what you're building. That's the problem.

You've told yourself you'll take Sunday off. Then Sunday morning comes and you're "just checking one thing." By noon you're deep in a rabbit hole. By evening you're wondering where the day went.

You know the cost. Conversations where you're not really there. Friends you keep rescheduling. Relationships, hobbies, health — all quietly withering while you ship.

You don't need more willpower. You need a lock you can't pick.

Everyone says life's a marathon. This is for those running it as a series of all-out sprints with blackouts in between.

How it works

Set it once. Mean it always.

Sunday night, you set your schedule for the week. Which apps. Which sites. Which hours. Then Fence holds the line.

Live your life

The version of you that set the rules wins. Not the version that wakes up ready to negotiate.

Why I built this

A tool I needed myself

I'm Vishal, a researcher who kept missing life while building things I loved.

I'd look up from my laptop and realize I'd been half-listening to my mum for twenty minutes. I'd skip the gym "just today" for six months straight. I'd tell myself I'd take Sunday off, then watch Sunday disappear into "one more feature."

The problem was never time. It was that I couldn't stop. And every blocker I tried was too easy to undo.

So I built Fence. The tool I needed. Maybe you need it too.
